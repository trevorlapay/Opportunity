## About Me

Justyna is an organizational transformation leader with a track record of walking into complexity and building the systems that make ambitious strategies real.
Her career spans two very different kinds of scale. At Equinix, a Fortune 500 global digital infrastructure company operating in over 70 markets, she served as Senior Director of Information Security Programs and Resiliency, owning the strategy-to-execution architecture of the organization's largest transformation programs, including a $70M portfolio of initiatives spanning AI security, identity management, and data protection. At Project ECHO, a healthcare nonprofit operating globally, she served as Chief Technology Officer and executive team member during a 500% growth phase, building the operating model from scratch and helping secure over $100M in philanthropic funding from the Gates, Rockefeller, Skoll, and MacArthur Foundations.
What makes her work distinctive is where she focuses: not on the technology or the strategy, but on the human and organizational layer where transformation most often breaks down. She is known for diagnosing the real problem rather than the stated one, getting siloed teams to move in the same direction, and building the trust and accountability structures that make change stick rather than stall.
Justyna operates across cultures fluently, speaks Danish, Polish, and English natively, and has led teams across the US, India, the Philippines, and Europe. She is currently exploring Chief of Staff, Head of Transformation, and VP of Strategy and Operations opportunities at mid-size organizations navigating significant change.
